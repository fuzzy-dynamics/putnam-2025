# Putnam 2025 B5 - Solution

## Answer

The number of integers $k \in \{1, \ldots, p-2\}$ such that $I(k+1) < I(k)$ is greater than $\frac{p}{4} - 1$.

## Solution

Let $D$ denote the number of **descents**, i.e., positions $k \in \{1, \ldots, p-2\}$ where $I(k+1) < I(k)$.

### Step 1: Counting Descents via Partial Sums

For $k \in \{1, \ldots, p-2\}$, define $f(k) = -\frac{1}{k(k+1)} \bmod p$, taking the representative in $\{1, 2, \ldots, p-1\}$.

Define partial sums $S_0 = 1$ and $S_m = 1 + \sum_{k=1}^{m} f(k)$ for $m = 1, \ldots, p-2$.

**Lemma 1:** $D = \lfloor S_{p-2} / p \rfloor$.

*Proof:* By partial fractions, $\frac{1}{k(k+1)} = \frac{1}{k} - \frac{1}{k+1}$, giving $S_m \equiv I(m+1) \pmod{p}$. Since each $f(k) \in \{1, \ldots, p-1\}$, the sequence $S_0 < S_1 < \cdots < S_{p-2}$ is strictly increasing. A descent at position $m$ means $I(m+1) < I(m)$, which occurs precisely when a multiple of $p$ lies in the interval $(S_{m-1}, S_m]$. The number of such multiples equals $\lfloor S_{p-2} / p \rfloor$.

### Step 2: Structural Classification

We classify the indices $\{1, \ldots, p-2\}$ into three types:

**Middle element:** $a_0 = \frac{p-1}{2}$ has $f(a_0) = 4$ since $a_0(a_0+1) = \frac{p^2-1}{4} \equiv -\frac{1}{4} \pmod{p}$.

**Fixed points:** An index $a$ is a *fixed point* if $a = \frac{-a-1}{2a+1} \bmod p$, equivalently $2a^2 + 2a + 1 \equiv 0 \pmod{p}$. This quadratic has solutions iff $-1$ is a quadratic residue mod $p$, i.e., iff $p \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$. In this case there are exactly 2 fixed points.

**Paired elements:** For $a$ neither middle nor fixed point, define $b = \frac{-a-1}{2a+1} \bmod p$. Then $b \in \{1, \ldots, p-2\}$, $b \neq a$, and:
$$f(a) + f(b) \equiv 4 \pmod{p}$$

*Proof of pairing formula:* Computing $b(b+1) = \frac{-a(a+1)}{(2a+1)^2}$, we get:
$$\frac{1}{a(a+1)} + \frac{1}{b(b+1)} = \frac{1 - (2a+1)^2}{a(a+1)} = -4$$
Thus $f(a) + f(b) \equiv 4 \pmod{p}$.

### Step 3: Bounding Pairs with Sum 4

Since $f(a), f(b) \in \{1, \ldots, p-1\}$ and $f(a) + f(b) \equiv 4 \pmod{p}$, we have $f(a) + f(b) \in \{4, p+4\}$.

**Lemma 2:** At most 2 pairs $(a, b)$ have $f(a) + f(b) = 4$.

*Proof:* For $f(a) + f(b) = 4$, we need $f(a), f(b) \in \{1, 2, 3\}$. Each value $v \in \{1, 2, 3\}$ arises as $f(k)$ for at most 2 values of $k$ (since $f(k) = f(m)$ implies $k(k+1) \equiv m(m+1) \pmod{p}$, giving $k = m$ or $m = p-1-k$).

Thus at most 6 indices have $f(k) \leq 3$. However, fixed points among these cannot pair with each other (they are self-paired). When $p \equiv 1 \pmod 4$, the two fixed points satisfy $f(a) = 2$ (since they solve $2a^2 + 2a + 1 = 0$, giving $a(a+1) = -1/2$, so $f(a) = 2$). Thus at least 2 of the small-$f$ values are fixed points, leaving at most 4 to form pairs, giving at most 2 pairs with sum 4.

### Step 4: Lower Bound on D

Let $m$ be the number of fixed points (0 if $p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$, 2 if $p \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$).

The $p-2$ indices partition as:
- 1 middle element (contributing $f(a_0) = 4$)
- $m$ fixed points (each contributing at least 1)
- $\frac{p-3-m}{2}$ pairs

Let $x \leq 2$ be the number of pairs with sum 4. The remaining pairs have sum $p + 4$.

$$\sum_{k=1}^{p-2} f(k) = 4 + \sum_{\text{fixed}} f(a) + 4x + (p+4)\left(\frac{p-3-m}{2} - x\right)$$

$$\geq 4 + m + (p+4) \cdot \frac{p-3-m}{2} - px$$

Since $x \leq 2$:
$$\sum f(k) \geq 4 + m + (p+4) \cdot \frac{p-3-m}{2} - 2p$$

**Case $p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$ (so $m = 0$):**
$$\sum f(k) \geq 4 + (p+4) \cdot \frac{p-3}{2} - 2p = 4 + \frac{p^2 + p - 12}{2} - 2p = \frac{p^2 - 3p - 4}{2}$$

$$S_{p-2} = 1 + \sum f(k) \geq \frac{p^2 - 3p - 2}{2}$$

$$D \geq \left\lfloor \frac{p^2 - 3p - 2}{2p} \right\rfloor = \left\lfloor \frac{p - 3}{2} - \frac{1}{p} \right\rfloor = \frac{p - 5}{2} \text{ for } p \geq 7$$

For $D > \frac{p}{4} - 1$: $\frac{p-5}{2} > \frac{p-4}{4}$ gives $2(p-5) > p-4$, i.e., $p > 6$. So this holds for $p \geq 7$.

**Case $p \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$ (so $m = 2$):**
$$\sum f(k) \geq 4 + 2 + (p+4) \cdot \frac{p-5}{2} - 2p = 6 + \frac{p^2 - p - 20}{2} - 2p = \frac{p^2 - 5p - 8}{2}$$

$$S_{p-2} \geq \frac{p^2 - 5p - 6}{2}$$

$$D \geq \left\lfloor \frac{p - 5}{2} - \frac{3}{p} \right\rfloor = \frac{p - 7}{2} \text{ for } p \geq 13$$

For $D > \frac{p}{4} - 1$: $\frac{p-7}{2} > \frac{p-4}{4}$ gives $p > 10$. So this holds for $p \geq 13$.

**Small cases:** For $p = 5$: $I = (1, 3, 2, 4)$, so $D = 1 > 0.25$. This verifies the bound.

**Conclusion:** For all primes $p > 3$, we have $D > \frac{p}{4} - 1$. QED
